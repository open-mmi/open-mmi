# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in the Open MMI project a respectful, harassment-free experience for everyone.

Open MMI is a technical project involving Linux, vehicle CAN buses, reverse engineering, dashboards, and vehicle integrations. Discussions may involve safety-sensitive topics, so respectful, careful communication is expected.

## Expected Behaviour

Examples of behaviour that contributes to a positive environment:

- being respectful of differing experience levels
- giving constructive technical feedback
- keeping safety in mind when discussing vehicle integrations
- documenting assumptions clearly
- helping contributors keep vehicle-specific knowledge inside profiles rather than core code
- being patient with users who are new to Linux, CAN, or automotive reverse engineering

## Unacceptable Behaviour

Examples of unacceptable behaviour include:

- harassment, insults, or personal attacks
- publishing unsafe instructions without appropriate warnings
- encouraging dangerous in-vehicle testing
- pressuring others to test unverified changes on public roads
- intentionally submitting misleading vehicle status mappings
- sharing private contact details or sensitive information without permission

## Vehicle Safety

Open MMI currently focuses on passive CAN receive, local Linux actions, status decoding, and UI/dashboard consumers.

Contributors should avoid encouraging unsafe behaviour, especially anything that could distract a driver, interfere with vehicle-critical systems, or imply a feature is safe before it has been properly reviewed and tested.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may remove comments, issues, pull requests, or contributions that violate this Code of Conduct.

Maintainers may also temporarily or permanently ban contributors for behaviour that is harmful to the project or community.

## Reporting

To report a concern, open a private security/safety report if appropriate, or contact the project maintainer through GitHub.

Reports will be reviewed with care and confidentiality where possible.
